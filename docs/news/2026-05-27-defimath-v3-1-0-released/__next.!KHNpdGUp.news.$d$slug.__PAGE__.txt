4:T922,<p>A focused minor release adding <a href="/docs/math/cbrt"><code>cbrt</code></a> — the missing
primitive for cube roots in 18-decimal fixed-point — implemented with the
same CLZ + Newton-Raphson recipe that powers <a href="/docs/math/sqrt"><code>sqrt</code></a>.</p>
<h2>New: <code>cbrt</code></h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>368 gas average</strong> on a 1e-4 → 1e4 sweep</li>
<li><strong>~33% cheaper</strong> than Solady's <code>cbrtWad</code> (550 gas) at identical
precision (both bit-exact at the ulp limit, max rel error 2.2e-14%)</li>
<li><strong>6 Newton iterations</strong> from a CLZ-derived initial guess
(<code>y₀ = 2^⌈bits/3⌉</code>), within factor ∛2 of the true root</li>
<li><strong>Domain</strong>: <code>0 ≤ x &#x3C; 2⁷⁶ ≈ 7.6e22</code> in 18-decimal fixed-point;
reverts with <code>CbrtUpperBoundError</code> above that</li>
<li><strong>Single-branch</strong> implementation: <code>result = integer_cbrt(x · 1e36)</code>
works symmetrically across the full domain, no inversion trick needed</li>
</ul>

<p>Like the rest of DeFiMath, <code>cbrt</code> is <code>internal pure</code>, no storage access,
no external calls.</p>
<h2>Why it matters</h2>
<p>Cube roots show up in higher-order risk math (skew, kurtosis adjustments),
Cardano's formula for cubic equations, and a handful of volatility models.
Until now the only on-chain option was Solady's <code>cbrtWad</code>; DeFiMath gives
the same primitive at a third less gas with no precision trade-off.</p>
