4:T95e,<p>DeFiMath now supports binary (cash-or-nothing) options end-to-end, with both
call and put pricing plus the full Greek set. All functions assume a unit
payout of 1 — scale the result for any payout size.</p>
<p><strong>New functions:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong><code>binaryCallPrice</code></strong> — Black-Scholes price of a binary call (pays 1 if
spot > strike at expiry, else 0). Computes <code>e^(-r·τ) · Φ(d2)</code>.</li>
<li><strong><code>binaryPutPrice</code></strong> — Black-Scholes price of a binary put (pays 1 if
spot &#x3C; strike at expiry, else 0).</li>
<li><strong><code>binaryDelta</code></strong> — first-order sensitivity to the underlying price.
Returns both call and put delta in a single call.</li>
<li><strong><code>binaryGamma</code></strong> — second-order sensitivity to the underlying price
(rate of change of delta). Returns both call and put.</li>
<li><strong><code>binaryTheta</code></strong> — sensitivity to time, expressed per day. Returns both
call and put.</li>
<li><strong><code>binaryVega</code></strong> — sensitivity to volatility, expressed per 1% change
in vol. Returns both call and put.</li>
</ul>
<p>This rounds out the binary options surface alongside the existing vanilla
options support.</p>
<h2>Use cases</h2>
<p>Binary options are the natural pricing primitive for any discrete, yes/no
payoff. On-chain, the most obvious application is <strong>prediction markets</strong>
— fair-value pricing for binary outcome shares
(e.g. "will price > X at time T?") on Polymarket-style protocols. The
same math underpins <strong>depeg and catastrophic-event insurance</strong>: protocols
that pay out a fixed amount if a stablecoin breaks its peg, an oracle
price crosses a threshold, or a similar discrete event occurs.</p>
<p>Beyond that, binary pricing is what <strong>structured yield products</strong> need
when they sell binaries to collect premium income — price and Greeks
drive the risk management. Binaries also work as <strong>threshold-based
hedges</strong> against specific triggers (liquidation levels, oracle bands,
knock-out barriers) and as the pricing layer for <strong>conditional rewards
and incentives</strong> that depend on an observable on-chain condition being
met by a deadline.</p>
