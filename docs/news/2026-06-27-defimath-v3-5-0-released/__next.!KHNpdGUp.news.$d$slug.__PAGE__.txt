4:Td3d,<p>Three input ceilings widened to the actual mathematical limit of each function.
Same precision, same gas, no API change. The previous bounds were conservative
defaults — chosen to match typical 2024 on-chain use. Long-dated insurance,
RWA bond options, and primitives composing on top of <code>sqrt</code> over very large
numbers now fit without recompiling.</p>
<h2>sqrt: full FP18-safe range</h2>
<p><code>SQRT_UPPER_BOUND</code> on the <a href="/docs/math/sqrt/">sqrt function</a> raised from
<code>2^80 · 1e18 ≈ 1.21 × 10⁴²</code> to <code>⌊(2²⁵⁶ − 1) / 1e18⌋ + 1 ≈ 1.158 × 10⁵⁹</code> —
a ~10¹⁷× increase. The new ceiling is exactly the boundary where the FP18
scaling step <code>x · 1e18</code> would overflow uint256; sqrt now exposes its full
precision-safe domain to callers.</p>
<h2>Options &#x26; Binary: MAX_EXPIRATION 2y → 32y</h2>
<p><code>MAX_EXPIRATION</code> in both the <a href="/docs/options/">Options</a> and
<a href="/docs/binary/">Binary</a> modules raised from <strong>2 years</strong> to <strong>32 years</strong>:</p>
<pre class="shiki github-dark" style="background-color:#24292e;color:#e1e4e8" tabindex="0"><code><span class="line"><span style="color:#6A737D">// Options.sol and Binary.sol</span></span>
<span class="line"><span style="color:#79B8FF">uint256</span><span style="color:#F97583"> internal</span><span style="color:#F97583"> constant</span><span style="color:#E1E4E8"> MAX_EXPIRATION </span><span style="color:#F97583">=</span><span style="color:#79B8FF"> 32</span><span style="color:#F97583"> *</span><span style="color:#E1E4E8"> SECONDS_IN_YEAR </span><span style="color:#F97583">+</span><span style="color:#79B8FF"> 1</span><span style="color:#E1E4E8">;</span></span></code></pre>
<p>(Was <code>63072000 + 1</code>.) <code>timeToExp = 1,009,152,000</code> (exactly 32 years) is now
accepted; anything above reverts with <code>TimeToExpiryUpperBoundError</code>.
<code>MAX_RATE = 400%</code> and every other input bound is unchanged.</p>
<h3>Why 32 years</h3>
<p>The hard math limit is <code>scaledRate = rate · timeYear &#x3C; EXP_UPPER_BOUND ≈ 135.3</code>
(that's where <code>expPositive</code> is precision-tuned). At <code>rate = MAX_RATE = 4</code>, the
maximum allowable horizon is <code>135.3 / 4 ≈ 33.8 years</code>. 32 years sits ~5%
under that ceiling — wide enough to admit every realistic long-dated product,
with margin against precision degradation at the corner. At the worst
combined corner (<code>rate = 400%</code>, <code>vol = uint64.max ≈ 1844%</code>, <code>T = 32y</code>), both
<code>scaledRate = 128</code> and <code>scaledVol = vol · √T ≈ 104</code> stay under <code>135.3</code>.</p>
<h3>Precision at the extended corners</h3>
<p>Worst observed absolute error across the new extended-bound sweep (4y, 8y,
30y, 50y horizons; 1000% vol; 200% rate; far-OTM and deep-ITM strikes) on a
$1,000-spot call: <strong>5.9 × 10⁻¹² $</strong> — over 20× under the standard
<code>MAX_OPTION_ABS_ERROR = 1.3 × 10⁻¹⁰</code> threshold.</p>
