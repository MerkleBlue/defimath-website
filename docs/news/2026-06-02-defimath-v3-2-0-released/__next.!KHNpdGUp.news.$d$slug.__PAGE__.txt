4:Tbfc,<p>Seven small utility math functions land in <a href="/docs/math">the Math module</a> — <code>mulDiv</code>,
<code>mul</code>, <code>abs</code>, <code>min</code>, <code>max</code>, <code>clamp</code>, <code>avg</code>. The kind every DeFi protocol ends up
reimplementing, often without enough care. Each one is branchless where the math
allows, gas-tight, and validated against BigInt references over 500+ random inputs
per function. They sit alongside the existing exponential/log, root, and probability
primitives in the same <code>DeFiMath</code> library — <code>internal pure</code>, inlined into your
contract by the Solidity compiler, no external <code>CALL</code> overhead.</p>
<h2><code>mulDiv</code> and <code>mul</code></h2>
<p><code>mulDiv(a, b, d)</code> computes <code>a · b / d</code> with full 512-bit
intermediate precision — the Remco Bloemen / Uniswap V3 algorithm that splits the
512-bit product into two 256-bit words via the <code>mulmod</code> trick, then does 512-by-256
division using Newton-Raphson modular inverse. Reverts with named errors on
<code>d == 0</code> (<code>MulDivByZeroError</code>) or quotient overflow (<code>MulDivOverflowError</code>).</p>
<p><code>mul(a, b)</code> is a specialization for the <code>1e18</code> denominator case —
the most common fixed-point multiply in DeFi. All denominator-dependent constants
are precomputed: the <code>2^18</code> factor in <code>1e18 = 2^18 · 5^18</code> becomes a shift, the
reduced odd denominator <code>5^18</code> is a literal, and the modular inverse mod <code>2^256</code>
is baked into the bytecode as <code>0xaccb…0669</code>.</p>
<p>Realistic-input measurements (500 random pairs across 1e6..1e30):</p>
<ul>
<li><code>mulDiv(a, b, 1e18)</code>: <strong>155 gas</strong></li>
<li><code>mul(a, b)</code>: <strong>130 gas</strong> — ~16% cheaper, same precision</li>
</ul>
<h2>Bit-tight utilities</h2>
<p>The remaining five round out the basics:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>abs(int256)</code> — <strong>17 gas</strong>, branchless via <code>SAR + XOR + SUB</code>. Handles <code>type(int256).min</code> cleanly via two's-complement wrap.</li>
<li><code>min(a, b)</code> / <code>max(a, b)</code> — <strong>23 gas each</strong>, three-opcode branchless: <code>x XOR ((x XOR y) · LT/GT)</code>.</li>
<li><code>clamp(x, lo, hi)</code> — <strong>78 gas</strong>, composed <code>max(x, lo)</code> then <code>min(_, hi)</code>.</li>
<li><code>avg(a, b)</code> — <strong>21 gas</strong>, overflow-safe <code>(a &#x26; b) + ((a ^ b) >> 1)</code>. Never wraps even at the <code>uint256</code> ceiling where naive <code>(a + b) / 2</code> would.</li>
</ul>
<p>All seven are documented on the <a href="/docs/math">Math module page</a>.</p>
